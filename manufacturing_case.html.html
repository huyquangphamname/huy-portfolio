Connecting Operations to Data Systems

In a manufacturing environment (Nam Thuan), raw output is meaningless without data context. My role was to engineer the "Physical API" of the factory. By mapping manual workflows into standardized BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation), I created the structure necessary for automation systems to ingest production results.

Before: The "Black Box"

Workshops operated on tribal knowledge. Defect roots were untraceable because processes varied by shift. No clean data for ERP systems.

After: The "Glass Factory"

Processes mapped in BPMN. Critical Quality Points (CTQ) defined as data entry nodes. Ready for full automated logging.

Identify the Bottleneck

Using Time & Motion studies, I identified that material staging accounted for 30% of downtime.

Redesigning the Workflow

I utilized BPMN to redesign the flow, integrating Quality Checkpoints that serve as data collection triggers.

  • Step 1: Standardized input materials to reduce variation.
  • Step 2: Implemented "Digital Handshakes" between inventory and shop floor.
  • Step 3: Created visual SOPs to ensure 100% compliance.

Throughput stability significantly improved post-optimization.
